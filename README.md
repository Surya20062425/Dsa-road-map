# Dsa-road-map
You can learn DSA completely for free — here’s a practical, no-cost plan (resources + schedule) you can follow using GeeksforGeeks and other free sites.

Start guide (direct answer): Follow GFG’s free DSA roadmap and practice problems, pair it with free LeetCode/Github problem lists and CodeChef/Codeforces contests for speed and variety.

Week-by-week 8-week free plan:

Week 1 — Foundations: Learn time/space complexity, arrays, and strings; read GFG tutorials and solve 20 easy array/string problems on GFG practice.

Week 2 — Two-pointers & Sorting: Study two-pointers, sliding window, and basic sorting (merge/quick); solve 15 problems (mix easy/medium) on GFG and LeetCode free tier.

Week 3 — Linked Lists & Stacks/Queues: Learn implementations and common patterns; solve 15 problems on GFG practice and reimplement each solution.

Week 4 — Recursion & Binary Search: Master recursion patterns and binary search templates; solve 12–15 problems and track recurring patterns.

Week 5 — Trees (BSTs): Study traversals, tree construction, and basic tree problems; solve 12 medium problems on GFG and LeetCode.

Week 6 — Graphs (BFS/DFS): Learn adjacency lists, BFS, DFS, shortest-path basics; solve 10–12 graph problems and read editorials after attempts.

Week 7 — Hashing & Heaps: Practice hashmaps, sets, and priority-queue problems; solve 10 medium problems and implement key templates.

Week 8 — Dynamic Programming basics: Learn memoization and tabulation with classic problems (knapsack, LIS); solve 10 DP problems (start easy → medium).

Free resources to use:

GeeksforGeeks Roadmap & Practice — stepwise tutorials, topic-wise problems, editorial solutions, videos.

LeetCode (free tier) — company-tagged problems and difficulty filters for interview-style practice.

CodeChef Practice & contests — many free practice problems and monthly contests to build speed.

Codeforces — frequent free contests to sharpen algorithmic thinking and optimization.

freeCodeCamp, W3Schools videos/articles — free concept refreshers and exercises.

YouTube GFG playlists — free guided lesson series and 90-day roadmaps to follow along.

Practice habits (short list):

Solve daily: 1–2 problems on weekdays, 3–4 on weekends.

Attempt first, then read editorial, then reimplement without help.

Keep a cheat-sheet of templates (binary search, two-pointers, DFS/BFS, sliding window, DP states).

Maintain a GitHub repo with your solutions and short notes for revision.
